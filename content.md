INTRODUCTION 

Museums play a vital role in preserving, interpreting, and disseminating cultural heritage while serving as educational spaces for diverse audiences. Institutions such as the Colombo National Museum house extensive collections that reflect historical, cultural, and artistic significance. However, traditional museum environments primarily based
on static displays and textual descriptions often struggle to meet the expectations of modern visitors who are accustomed to interactive, personalized, and technology-driven experiences.
To address these challenges, there is a growing need for intelligent, integrated systems that combine personalization, accessibility, navigation, interactivity, and real-time feedback. This research proposes an AI-driven Smart Museum Guide system for the Colombo National Museum that unifies multiple technologies into a single platform. The system integrates QR-based artifact access with role-based personalization, AI-powered
2D reconstruction and 3D visualization, adaptive indoor navigation, and an emotionbased feedback system supported by a conversational chatbot.

Problem Statement & Stakeholders

A large public museum environment where local and international visitors explore artifacts through traditional static displays and limited digital interaction
Primary Users →  Museum visitors, Students and educational groups
Secondary Users → Museum staff, Tour guides, Cultural heritage researchers
Decision Makers → Museum management , Department of National Museums

Research Problem

The core problem lies in the absence of a unified, intelligent, and adaptive smart
museum system that can simultaneously address personalization, accessibility, navigation,
and feedback. Existing solutions tend to focus on individual components rather than
delivering a comprehensive platform that integrates these functionalities.
Therefore, this research aims to address the problem of designing and implementing an
AI-driven, integrated smart museum guide system that combines QR-based artifact
access, role-based personalized content, multilingual support, intelligent
recommendations, interactive 2D/3D visualization, adaptive indoor navigation, and
emotion-aware feedback to enhance visitor experience and support data-driven museum
management

Research Objectives

Main objective
The main objective of this research is to design and develop an integrated AI-driven Smart Museum Guide system that enhances visitor engagement, accessibility, and personalization through QR-based artifact access, intelligent recommendations, interactive visualization, adaptive indoor navigation, and emotion-aware feedback mechanisms for the Colombo National Museum.
Specific objectives
* To develop a QR-based artifact access system that enables visitors to retrieve
digital information of museum artifacts through unique QR codes linked to a
centralized backend system.
* To design a role-based personalized content delivery mechanism that adapts artifact descriptions according to visitor categories such as tourist, student, teacher, professor, and citizen.
* To implement a multilingual translation module using NLP-based APIs to provide real-time translation of artifact descriptions into multiple languages, improving accessibility for diverse users.
* To develop an AI-driven artifact classification and recommendation system using
a MobileNetV2-based model to classify artifacts and suggest related exhibits and
hidden gems based on user interaction and metadata.
* To design and integrate an interactive 2D and 3D artifact visualization system
using deep learning-based reconstruction and generative models to enhance
artifact accessibility without physical interaction.
* To develop an adaptive indoor navigation system using BLE beacon positioning,
sensor data, and GIS-based mapping to provide dynamic and context-aware route
optimization inside the museum.
* To implement an emotion-based feedback and chatbot system that captures facial
and vocal expressions to analyze visitor emotions and provide multilingual
conversational support using AI-based models.
* To integrate all components into a unified mobile application and backend
architecture to ensure seamless interaction, scalability, and real-time data
processing for smart museum operations.

METHODOLOGY

This research adopts a design science and machine learning–driven development
methodology to build an intelligent Smart Museum Guide system. The system integrates
multiple AI components including recommendation systems, multilingual translation,
indoor navigation, and emotion-aware feedback analysis. The methodology follows an
iterative lifecycle consisting of requirement analysis, data collection, system design,
model development, implementation, testing, and evaluation.
